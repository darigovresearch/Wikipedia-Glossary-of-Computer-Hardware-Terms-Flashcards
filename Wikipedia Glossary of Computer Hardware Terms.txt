"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conventional_PCI"" title=""Conventional PCI"">Conventional Peripheral Component Interconnect</a> (Conventional PCI)"|"<span id=""PCI""></span><span id=""pci""></span><span id=""Conventional_Peripheral_Component_Interconnect""></span>
<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also simply <b>PCI</b>.</span></p>
A computer <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#bus""><span title=""See entry on this page at § bus"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">bus</span></a> for attaching hardware devices in a computer.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CPU"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""CPU"">core</a>"|"
The portion of the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#cPU""><span title=""See entry on this page at § CPU"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">CPU</span></a> which actually performs <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arithmetic"" title=""Arithmetic"">arithmetic</a> and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logical_operations"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Logical operations"">logical operations</a>; many CPUs have multiple cores (e.g. ""a quad-core processor"").
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Core_memory"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Core memory"">core memory</a>"|"

"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CPU"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""CPU"">Central Processing Unit</a> (CPU)"|"<span id=""cpu""></span><span id=""Central_Processing_Unit""></span><span id=""central_processing_unit""></span>

"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Video_Disc"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Digital Video Disc"">Digital Video Disc</a> (DVD)"|"

"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Visual_Interface"" title=""Digital Visual Interface"">Digital Visual Interface</a> (DVI)"|"
A video display interface developed by the Digital Display Working Group (DDWG). The digital interface is used to connect a video source to a display device, such as a computer monitor.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_Access_Storage_Device"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Direct Access Storage Device"">Direct Access Storage Device</a> (DASD)"|"
A mainframe terminology introduced by IBM denoting secondary storage with random access, typically (arrays of) <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#hard_disk_drive""><span title=""See entry on this page at § hard disk drive"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">hard disk drives</span></a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_mapped_cache"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Direct mapped cache"">direct mapped cache</a>"|"
A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#cache""><span title=""See entry on this page at § cache"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">cache</span></a> where each <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#physical_address""><span title=""See entry on this page at § physical address"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">physical address</span></a> may only be mapped to one <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#cache_line""><span title=""See entry on this page at § cache line"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">cache line</span></a>, indexed using the low bits of the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#address""><span title=""See entry on this page at § address"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">address</span></a>. Simple but highly prone to allocation conflicts.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Direct_memory_access"" title=""Direct memory access"">direct memory access</a> (DMA)"|"
The ability of a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hardware_device&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1"" class=""new"" title=""Hardware device (page does not exist)"">hardware device</a> such as a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#disk_drive""><span title=""See entry on this page at § disk drive"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">disk drive</span></a> or <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_interface"" title=""Network interface"">network interface</a> to access <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#main_memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § main memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">main memory</span></a> without intervention from the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#cPU""><span title=""See entry on this page at § CPU"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">CPU</span></a>,  provided by one or more <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DMA_channel"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""DMA channel"">DMA channels</a> in a system.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DisplayPort"" title=""DisplayPort"">DisplayPort</a>"|"
A digital display interface developed by the Video Electronics Standards Association (VESA). The interface is primarily used to connect a video source to a display device such as a computer monitor, though it can also be used to transmit audio, USB, and other forms of data.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drive_bay"" title=""Drive bay"">drive bay</a>"|"
A standard-sized area within a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#computer_case""><span title=""See entry on this page at § computer case"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">computer case</span></a> for adding <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#hardware""><span title=""See entry on this page at § hardware"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">hardware</span></a> (hard drives, CD drives, etc.) to a computer.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DIMM"" title=""DIMM"">dual in-line memory module</a> (DIMM)"|"
A series of dynamic random-access memory integrated circuits. These modules are mounted on a printed circuit board and designed for use in personal computers, workstations and servers. Contrast <i><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#sIMM""><span title=""See entry on this page at § SIMM"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">SIMM</span></a></i>.
"
"dual issue"|"
A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superscalar"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Superscalar"">superscalar</a> pipeline capable of executing two instructions simultaneously.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamic_random-access_memory"" title=""Dynamic random-access memory"">dynamic random-access memory</a> (DRAM)"|"
A type of random-access memory that stores each bit of data in a separate capacitor within an integrated circuit and which must be periodically refreshed to retain the stored data.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expansion_bus"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Expansion bus"">expansion bus</a>"|"
A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#bus""><span title=""See entry on this page at § bus"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">computer bus</span></a> which moves information between the internal hardware of a computer system (including the CPU and RAM) and peripheral devices. It is a collection of wires and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communications_protocol"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Communications protocol"">protocols</a> that allows for the expansion of a computer.

<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § Memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">memory</span></a> that can retain the stored data even when not powered, as opposed to <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#volatile_memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § volatile memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">volatile memory</span></a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-volatile_random-access_memory"" title=""Non-volatile random-access memory"">non-volatile random-access memory</a>"|"
<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#random-access_memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § random-access memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">Random-access memory</span></a> (RAM) that retains its data when power is turned off.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operating_system"" title=""Operating system"">operating system</a>"|"
The set of software that manages computer <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#hardware_resource""><span title=""See entry on this page at § hardware resource"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">hardware resources</span></a> and provides common services for <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_program"" title=""Computer program"">computer programs</a>, typically loaded by the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BIOS"" title=""BIOS"">BIOS</a> on <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Booting"" title=""Booting"">booting</a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opcode"" title=""Opcode"">operation code</a>"|"
Several bits in a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_program"" title=""Computer program"">computer program</a> <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#instruction""><span title=""See entry on this page at § instruction"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">instruction</span></a> that specify which operation to perform.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_disc_drive"" title=""Optical disc drive"">optical disc drive</a>"|"
A type of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#disk_drive""><span title=""See entry on this page at § disk drive"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">disk drive</span></a> that uses <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laser_light"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Laser light"">laser light</a> or <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electromagnetic_wave"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Electromagnetic wave"">electromagnetic waves</a> near the <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Light_spectrum"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Light spectrum"">light spectrum</a> as part of the process of reading or writing data to or from <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_disc"" title=""Optical disc"">optical discs</a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pen_drive"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Pen drive"">pen drive</a>"|"
Another name for a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#uSB_flash_drive""><span title=""See entry on this page at § USB flash drive"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">USB flash drive</span></a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peripheral"" title=""Peripheral"">peripheral</a>"|"
Any device attached to a computer but not part of it.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_computer"" title=""Personal computer"">personal computer</a> (PC)"|"
Any <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General-purpose_computer"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""General-purpose computer"">general-purpose computer</a> whose size, capabilities, and original sales price make it useful for individuals, and which is intended to be operated directly by an end user, with no intervening computer operator.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_supply"" title=""Power supply"">power supply</a>"|"
A unit of the computer that converts mains AC to low-voltage regulated DC for the power of all the computer components.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_supply_unit_(computer)"" title=""Power supply unit (computer)"">power supply unit</a> (PSU)"|"
Converts <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mains_electricity"" title=""Mains electricity"">mains AC</a> to low-voltage regulated <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DC_power"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""DC power"">DC power</a> for the internal components of a computer. Modern personal computers universally use <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switched-mode_power_supply"" title=""Switched-mode power supply"">switched-mode power supplies</a>. Some <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#power_supply""><span title=""See entry on this page at § power supply"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">power supplies</span></a> have a manual switch for selecting input voltage, while others automatically adapt to the mains voltage.
"
"prefetch"|"
The process of pre-loading <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Instruction_(computing)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Instruction (computing)"">instructions</a> or data into a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#cache""><span title=""See entry on this page at § cache"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">cache</span></a> ahead of time, either under manual control via <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prefetch_instruction"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Prefetch instruction"">prefetch instructions</a> or automatically by a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Prefetch_unit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1"" class=""new"" title=""Prefetch unit (page does not exist)"">prefetch unit</a> which may use runtime heuristics to predict the future <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory_access_pattern"" title=""Memory access pattern"">memory access pattern</a>.
"
"prefetching"|"
The pre-loading of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Instructions_(computing)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1"" class=""new"" title=""Instructions (computing) (page does not exist)"">instructions</a> or <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_(computing)"" title=""Data (computing)"">data</a> before either is needed by dedicated <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cache_control_instruction"" title=""Cache control instruction"">cache control instructions</a> or predictive hardware, to mitigate <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latency_(engineering)"" title=""Latency (engineering)"">latency</a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printer_(computing)"" title=""Printer (computing)"">printer</a>"|"
A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#peripheral""><span title=""See entry on this page at § peripheral"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">peripheral</span></a> which produces a text or graphics of documents stored in electronic form, usually on <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Physical_print_media&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1"" class=""new"" title=""Physical print media (page does not exist)"">physical print media</a> such as paper or transparencies.
"
"process node"|"
Refers to a level of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor"" title=""Semiconductor"">semiconductor</a> <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor_device_fabrication"" title=""Semiconductor device fabrication"">manufacturing technology</a>, one of several successive <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transistor"" title=""Transistor"">transistor</a> shrinks.
"
"processing element"|"
An <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_circuit"" title=""Electronic circuit"">electronic circuit</a> (either a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microprocessor"" title=""Microprocessor"">microprocessor</a> or an internal component of one) that may function autonomously or under external control, performing <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arithmetic_and_logic_operation"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Arithmetic and logic operation"">arithmetic and logic operations</a> on <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Data_(computing)"" title=""Data (computing)"">data</a>, possibly containing <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#local_memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § local memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">local memory</span></a>, and possibly connected to other <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#processing_element"">processing elements</a> via a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_network"" title=""Computer network"">network</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_on_a_chip"" title=""Network on a chip"">network on a chip</a>, or <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cache_hierarchy"" title=""Cache hierarchy"">cache hierarchy</a>.
"
"processor node"|"
A <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Processor_(computing)"" title=""Processor (computing)"">processor</a> in a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Multiprocessor_system&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1"" class=""new"" title=""Multiprocessor system (page does not exist)"">multiprocessor system</a> or <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cluster_(computing)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Cluster (computing)"">cluster</a>, connected by dedicated communication channels or a <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_(computing)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Network (computing)"">network</a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programmable_read-only_memory"" title=""Programmable read-only memory"">programmable read-only memory</a> (PROM)"|"
A type of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-volatile_memory"" title=""Non-volatile memory"">non-volatile memory</a> chip that may be programmed after the device is constructed.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programmer_(hardware)"" title=""Programmer (hardware)"">programmer</a>"|"
Any electronic equipment that arranges written software to configure programmable <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-volatile_memory"" title=""Non-volatile memory"">non-volatile</a> <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Integrated_circuit"" title=""Integrated circuit"">integrated circuits</a> (called programmable devices) such as  <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EPROM"" title=""EPROM"">EPROMs</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EEPROM"" title=""EEPROM"">EEPROMs</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flash_Memory"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Flash Memory"">Flashes</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MultiMediaCard"" title=""MultiMediaCard"">eMMC</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magnetoresistive_random-access_memory"" title=""Magnetoresistive random-access memory"">MRAM</a>, FRAM, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-volatile_random-access_memory"" title=""Non-volatile random-access memory"">NV RAM</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programmable_Array_Logic"" title=""Programmable Array Logic"">PALs</a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Field_Programmable_Gate_Array"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Field Programmable Gate Array"">FPGAs</a> or <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Programmable_logic"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Programmable logic"">programmable logic</a> circuits.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PCI_Express"" title=""PCI Express"">PCI Express</a> (PCIe)"|"
An <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#expansion_bus""><span title=""See entry on this page at § expansion bus"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">expansion bus</span></a> standard designed to replace the older <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#pCI""><span title=""See entry on this page at § PCI"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">PCI</span></a>, <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#pCI-X""><span title=""See entry on this page at § PCI-X"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">PCI-X</span></a>, and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#aGP""><span title=""See entry on this page at § AGP"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">AGP</span></a> bus standards.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PCI-eXtended"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""PCI-eXtended"">PCI-eXtended</a> (PCI-X)"|"
An <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#expansion_bus""><span title=""See entry on this page at § expansion bus"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">expansion bus</span></a> and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#expansion_card""><span title=""See entry on this page at § expansion card"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">expansion card</span></a> standard that enhances the 32-bit PCI Local Bus for higher bandwidth demanded by servers.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Redundant_Array_of_Independent_Disks"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Redundant Array of Independent Disks"">Redundant Array of Independent Disks</a> (RAID)"|"
Any of various data storage schemes that can divide and replicate data across multiple hard disk drives in order to increase reliability, allow faster access, or both.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Random-access_memory"" title=""Random-access memory"">random-access memory</a> (RAM)"|"
A type of computer data storage that allows data items to be accessed (<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Read_(computer)"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Read (computer)"">read</a> or written) in almost the same amount of time irrespective of the physical location of data inside the memory. RAM contains <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiplexer"" title=""Multiplexer"">multiplexing</a> and <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demultiplexing"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Demultiplexing"">demultiplexing</a> circuitry to connect the data lines to the addressed storage for reading or writing the entry. Usually more than one bit of storage is accessed by the same address, and RAM devices often have multiple data lines and are said to be '8-bit' or '16-bit' etc. devices. In today's technology, random-access memory takes the form of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#integrated_circuit""><span title=""See entry on this page at § integrated circuit"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">integrated circuits</span></a>.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Read-only_memory"" title=""Read-only memory"">read-only memory</a> (ROM)"|"
A type of memory chip that retains its data when its power supply is switched off.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Server_(computing)"" title=""Server (computing)"">server</a>"|"
A computer which may be used to provide services to clients.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Software"" title=""Software"">software</a>"|"
Any <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Computer_program"" title=""Computer program"">computer program</a> or other kind of information that can be read and/or written by a computer.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Single_in-line_memory_module"" class=""mw-redirect"" title=""Single in-line memory module"">single in-line memory module</a> (SIMM)"|"
A type of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory_module"" title=""Memory module"">memory module</a> containing <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#random-access_memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § random-access memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">random-access memory</span></a> used in computers from the early 1980s to the late 1990s. Contrast <i><a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#dIMM""><span title=""See entry on this page at § DIMM"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">DIMM</span></a></i>.

<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also <b>solid-state disk</b> or <b>electronic disk</b>.</span></p>
Any data storage device that uses integrated circuit assemblies as memory to store data persistently. Though they are sometimes referred to as solid-state disks, these devices contain neither an actual disk nor a drive motor to spin a disk.
"
"<a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Static_random-access_memory"" title=""Static random-access memory"">static random-access memory</a> (SRAM)"|"
A type of <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductor"" title=""Semiconductor"">semiconductor</a> <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#memory""><span title=""See entry on this page at § memory"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">memory</span></a> that uses bistable latching circuitry to store each bit. The term static differentiates it from <a href=""https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms#dRAM""><span title=""See entry on this page at § DRAM"" class=""glossary-link-internal"" style=""border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;"" id="""">DRAM</span></a>, which must be periodically refreshed.
"

<p class=""glossary-hatnote""><span role=""note"" class=""hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref"">Also <b>audio card</b>.</span></p>
