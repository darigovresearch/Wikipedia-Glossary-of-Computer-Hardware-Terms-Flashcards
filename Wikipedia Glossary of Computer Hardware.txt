<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="A">A</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: A">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="accelerated_graphics_port_(agp)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Accelerated_Graphics_Port" title="Accelerated Graphics Port">Accelerated Graphics Port</a> (AGP)</dfn></dt><span id="agp"></span><span id="Accelerated_Graphics_Port"></span>
<dd class="glossary">A high-speed point-to-point channel for attaching a <a href="#video_card"><span title="See entry on this page at § video card" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">video card</span></a> to a computer's <a href="#motherboard"><span title="See entry on this page at § motherboard" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">motherboard</span></a>, primarily to assist in the acceleration of <a href="/wiki/3D_computer_graphics" title="3D computer graphics">3D computer graphics</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="accelerator" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Hardware_acceleration" title="Hardware acceleration">accelerator</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#microprocessor"><span title="See entry on this page at § microprocessor" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">microprocessor</span></a>, <a href="#aSIC"><span title="See entry on this page at § ASIC" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">ASIC</span></a>, or <a href="#expansion_card"><span title="See entry on this page at § expansion card" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">expansion card</span></a> designed to offload a specific task from the <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a>, often containing <a href="#fixed_function"><span title="See entry on this page at § fixed function" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">fixed function</span></a> hardware. A common example is a <a href="#gPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § GPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">graphics processing unit</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="accumulator" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Accumulator_(computing)" title="Accumulator (computing)">accumulator</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A register in a <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a> in which intermediate <a href="/wiki/Arithmetic" title="Arithmetic">arithmetic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Logic" title="Logic">logic</a> results are stored.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="address" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Address_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Address (computing)">address</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The unique <a href="/wiki/Integer" title="Integer">integer</a> number that specifies a <a href="#memory_location"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory location" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory location</span></a> in an <a href="#address_space"><span title="See entry on this page at § address space" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address space</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="address_space" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Address_space" title="Address space">address space</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A mapping of logical <a href="#address"><span title="See entry on this page at § address" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">addresses</span></a> into <a href="/wiki/Physical_memory" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical memory">physical memory</a> or other <a href="#memory-mapped_device"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory-mapped device" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory-mapped devices</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="advanced_technology_extended_(atx)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/ATX" title="ATX">Advanced Technology eXtended</a> (ATX)</dfn></dt><span id="atx"></span><span id="Advanced_Technology_Extended"></span><span id="Advanced_Technology_eXtended"></span>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#motherboard"><span title="See entry on this page at § motherboard" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">motherboard</span></a> <a href="#form_factor"><span title="See entry on this page at § form factor" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">form factor</span></a> specification developed by Intel in 1995 to improve on previous DE factor standards like the AT form factor.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="ai_accelerator" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/AI_accelerator" title="AI accelerator">AI accelerator</a></dfn></dt><span id="ai_accelerator"></span>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="#accelerator"><span title="See entry on this page at § accelerator" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">accelerator</span></a> aimed at running <a href="/wiki/Artificial_neural_network" title="Artificial neural network">artificial neural networks</a> or other <a href="/wiki/Machine_learning" title="Machine learning">machine learning</a> and <a href="/wiki/Machine_vision" title="Machine vision">machine vision</a> algorithms (either training or deployment), e.g. <a href="/wiki/Movidius_Myriad_2" class="mw-redirect" title="Movidius Myriad 2">Movidius Myriad 2</a>, <a href="/wiki/TrueNorth" class="mw-redirect" title="TrueNorth">TrueNorth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tensor_processing_unit" title="Tensor processing unit">tensor processing unit</a>, etc.</dd>

</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="B">B</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: B">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="blu-ray_disc_(bd)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Blu-ray_Disc" class="mw-redirect" title="Blu-ray Disc">Blu-ray Disc</a> (BD)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="#optical_disc"><span title="See entry on this page at § optical disc" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">optical disc</span></a> <a href="#storage_medium"><span title="See entry on this page at § storage medium" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">storage medium</span></a> designed to supersede the <a href="#dVD"><span title="See entry on this page at § DVD" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">DVD</span></a> format.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="bus" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Bus_(computing)" title="Bus (computing)">bus</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A subsystem that transfers data between computer components inside a computer or between computers.</dd>

</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="C">C</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: C">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="cache" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache_(computing)" title="Cache (computing)">cache</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A small, fast <a href="#local_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § local memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">local memory</span></a> that transparently buffers access to a larger but slower or more distant/higher latency <a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory</span></a>  or <a href="#storage_medium"><span title="See entry on this page at § storage medium" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">storage device</span></a>, organised into <a href="#cache_line"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache line" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache lines</span></a>. Automatically translates accesses to the underlying resources <a href="#address_space"><span title="See entry on this page at § address space" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address space</span></a> to locations in the cache.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_coherency" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache_coherency" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache coherency">cache coherency</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The process of keeping data in multiple <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">caches</span></a> synchronised in a <a href="#multiprocessor"><span title="See entry on this page at § multiprocessor" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">multiprocessor</span></a> <a href="#shared_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § shared memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">shared memory</span></a> system, also required when <a href="#dMA"><span title="See entry on this page at § DMA" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">DMA</span></a> modifies the underlying memory.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_eviction" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache_eviction" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache eviction">cache eviction</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Freeing up data from within a <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> to make room for new <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cache_entries&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cache entries (page does not exist)">cache entries</a> to be <a href="/w/index.php?title=Allocation_(computing)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Allocation (computing) (page does not exist)">allocated</a>; controlled by a <a href="/wiki/Cache_replacement_policy" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache replacement policy">cache replacement policy</a>. Caused by a <a href="#cache_miss"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache miss" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache miss</span></a> whilst a cache is already full.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_hit" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache_hit" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache hit">cache hit</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Finding <a href="/wiki/Data_(computing)" title="Data (computing)">data</a> in a local <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a>, preventing the need to search for that resource in a more distant location (or to repeat a calculation).</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_line" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/CPU_cache#CACHE-LINES" title="CPU cache">cache line</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A small block of <a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory</span></a> within a <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a>; the granularity of allocation, refills, eviction; typically 32–128 bytes in size.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_miss" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache_miss" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache miss">cache miss</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Not finding <a href="/wiki/Data_(computing)" title="Data (computing)">data</a> in a local <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a>, requiring use of the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cache_policy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Cache policy (page does not exist)">cache policy</a> to <a href="/w/index.php?title=Allocation_(computing)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Allocation (computing) (page does not exist)">allocate</a> and fill this data, and possibly performing <a href="/wiki/Cache_eviction" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache eviction">evicting</a> other data to make room.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_thrashing" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache_thrashing" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache thrashing">cache thrashing</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A pathological situation where access in a <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> cause cyclical <a href="/wiki/Cache_misses" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache misses">cache misses</a> by <a href="/wiki/Cache_eviction" class="mw-redirect" title="Cache eviction">evicting</a> data that is needed in the near future.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache_ways" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">cache ways</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The number of potential <a href="#cache_line"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache line" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache lines</span></a> in an <a href="/wiki/Associative_cache" class="mw-redirect" title="Associative cache">associative cache</a> that specific <a href="/w/index.php?title=Physical_addresses&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Physical addresses (page does not exist)">physical addresses</a> can be mapped to; higher values reduce potential collisions in allocation.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="cache-only_memory_architecture_(coma)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Cache-only_memory_architecture" title="Cache-only memory architecture">cache-only memory architecture</a> (COMA)</dfn></dt><span id="coma"></span><span id="Cache-only_memory_architecture"></span><span id="Cache-Only_Memory_Architecture"></span>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#multiprocessor"><span title="See entry on this page at § multiprocessor" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">multiprocessor</span></a> <a href="#memory_architecture"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory architecture" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory architecture</span></a> where an <a href="#address_space"><span title="See entry on this page at § address space" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address space</span></a> is dynamically shifted between <a href="#processor_node"><span title="See entry on this page at § processor node" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">processor nodes</span></a> based on demand.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="card_reader" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Card_reader" title="Card reader">card reader</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any data input device that reads data from a card-shaped <a href="#storage_medium"><span title="See entry on this page at § storage medium" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">storage medium</span></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Shelly_Vermaat_2008_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shelly_Vermaat_2008-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Kent_Lancour_2016_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kent_Lancour_2016-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="channel_i/o" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Channel_I/O" title="Channel I/O">channel I/O</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A generic term that refers to a high-performance input/output (I/O) architecture that is implemented in various forms on a number of computer architectures, especially on mainframe computers.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="chipset" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Chipset" title="Chipset">chipset</a></dfn></dt><span id="chip_set"></span>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>chip set</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">A group of integrated circuits, or chips, that are designed to work together. They are usually marketed as a single product.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="compact_disc-recordable_(cd-r)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/CD-R" title="CD-R">Compact Disc-Recordable</a> (CD-R)</dfn></dt><span id="cdr"></span><span id="CDR"></span>
<dd class="glossary">A variation of the optical <a href="#compact_disc"><span title="See entry on this page at § compact disc" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">compact disc</span></a> which can be written to once.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="compact_disc-rewritable_(cd-rw)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/CD-RW" title="CD-RW">Compact Disc-ReWritable</a> (CD-RW)</dfn></dt><span id="cdrw"></span><span id="CDRW"></span><span id="Compact_Disc-ReWritable"></span>
<dd class="glossary">A variation of the optical <a href="#compact_disc"><span title="See entry on this page at § compact disc" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">compact disc</span></a> which can be written to many times.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="compact_disc_read-only_memory_(cd-rom)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/CD-ROM" title="CD-ROM">Compact Disc Read-Only Memory</a> (CD-ROM)</dfn></dt><span id="Compact_Disc_Read-Only_Memory"></span>
<dd class="glossary">A pre-pressed <a href="#compact_disc"><span title="See entry on this page at § compact disc" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">compact disc</span></a> which contains data or music playback and which cannot be written to.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="computer_case" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_case" title="Computer case">computer case</a></dfn></dt><span id="chassis"></span><span id="cabinet"></span><span id="box"></span><span id="tower"></span><span id="enclosure"></span><span id="housing"></span><span id="system_unit"></span><span id="case"></span>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>chassis</b>, <b>cabinet</b>, <b>box</b>, <b>tower</b>, <b>enclosure</b>, <b>housing</b>, <b>system unit</b>, or simply <b>case</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">The enclosure that contains most of the components of a computer, usually excluding the display, keyboard, mouse, and various other peripherals.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="computer_fan" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_fan" title="Computer fan">computer fan</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="/wiki/Active_cooling" title="Active cooling">active cooling</a> system forcing <a href="/wiki/Airflow" title="Airflow">airflow</a> inside or around a <a href="#computer_case"><span title="See entry on this page at § computer case" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">computer case</span></a> using a <a href="/wiki/Fan_(machine)" title="Fan (machine)">fan</a> to cause <a href="/wiki/Air_cooling" title="Air cooling">air cooling</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="computer_form_factor" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_form_factor" title="Computer form factor">computer form factor</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The name used to denote the dimensions, power supply type, location of mounting holes, number of ports on the back panel, etc.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="computer_monitor" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_monitor" title="Computer monitor">computer monitor</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="/wiki/Electronic_visual_display" title="Electronic visual display">electronic visual display</a> for computers. A monitor usually comprises the <a href="/wiki/Display_device" title="Display device">display device</a>, circuitry, casing, and power supply. The display device in modern monitors is typically a <a href="/wiki/Thin_film_transistor_liquid_crystal_display" class="mw-redirect" title="Thin film transistor liquid crystal display">thin film transistor liquid crystal display</a> (TFT-LCD) or a flat panel <a href="/wiki/LED_display" title="LED display">LED display</a>, whereas older monitors used a <a href="/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube" class="mw-redirect" title="Cathode ray tube">cathode ray tube</a> (CRT).<sup id="cite_ref-Shelly_Vermaat_2008_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shelly_Vermaat_2008-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="control_store" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Control_store" title="Control store">control store</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The memory that stores the <a href="#microcode"><span title="See entry on this page at § microcode" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">microcode</span></a> of a <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="conventional_peripheral_component_interconnect_(conventional_pci)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Conventional_PCI" title="Conventional PCI">Conventional Peripheral Component Interconnect</a> (Conventional PCI)</dfn></dt><span id="PCI"></span><span id="pci"></span><span id="Conventional_Peripheral_Component_Interconnect"></span>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also simply <b>PCI</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">A computer <a href="#bus"><span title="See entry on this page at § bus" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">bus</span></a> for attaching hardware devices in a computer.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="core" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/CPU" class="mw-redirect" title="CPU">core</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The portion of the <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a> which actually performs <a href="/wiki/Arithmetic" title="Arithmetic">arithmetic</a> and <a href="/wiki/Logical_operations" class="mw-redirect" title="Logical operations">logical operations</a>; many CPUs have multiple cores (e.g. "a quad-core processor").</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="core_memory" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Core_memory" class="mw-redirect" title="Core memory">core memory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">In modern usage, a synonym for <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a>, dating back from the pre-semiconductor-chip times when the dominant main memory technology was <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_core_memory" class="mw-redirect" title="Magnetic core memory">magnetic core memory</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="central_processing_unit_(cpu)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/CPU" class="mw-redirect" title="CPU">Central Processing Unit</a> (CPU)</dfn></dt><span id="cpu"></span><span id="Central_Processing_Unit"></span><span id="central_processing_unit"></span>
<dd class="glossary">The portion of a computer system that executes the instructions of a computer program.</dd>

</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="D">D</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: D">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="data_cache_(d-cache)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Data_cache" class="mw-redirect" title="Data cache">data cache</a> (D-cache)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> in a <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a> or <a href="#gPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § GPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">GPU</span></a> servicing data <a href="/wiki/Load_and_store" class="mw-redirect" title="Load and store">load and store</a> requests, mirroring <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a> (or <a href="#vRAM"><span title="See entry on this page at § VRAM" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">VRAM</span></a> for a <a href="#gPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § GPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">GPU</span></a>).</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="data_storage" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_data_storage" title="Computer data storage">data storage</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A technology consisting of computer components and <a href="/wiki/Data_storage_device" class="mw-redirect" title="Data storage device">recording media</a> used to retain digital <a href="/wiki/Data_(computing)" title="Data (computing)">data</a>. It is a core function and fundamental component of computers.<sup id="cite_ref-Shelly_Vermaat_2008_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shelly_Vermaat_2008-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="device_memory" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">device memory</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary"><a href="#local_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § Local memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">local memory</span></a> associated with a hardware device such as a <a href="#gPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § GPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">graphics processing unit</span></a> or <a href="/wiki/OpenCL" title="OpenCL">OpenCL</a> <a href="/wiki/Compute_device" class="mw-redirect" title="Compute device">compute device</a>, distinct from <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="digital_video_disc_(dvd)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Digital_Video_Disc" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital Video Disc">Digital Video Disc</a> (DVD)</dfn></dt>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>Digital Versatile Disc</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">An optical compact disc - of the same dimensions as compact discs (CDs), but store more than six times as much data.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="digital_visual_interface_(dvi)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Digital_Visual_Interface" title="Digital Visual Interface">Digital Visual Interface</a> (DVI)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A video display interface developed by the Digital Display Working Group (DDWG). The digital interface is used to connect a video source to a display device, such as a computer monitor.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="direct_access_storage_device_(dasd)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Direct_Access_Storage_Device" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct Access Storage Device">Direct Access Storage Device</a> (DASD)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A mainframe terminology introduced by IBM denoting secondary storage with random access, typically (arrays of) <a href="#hard_disk_drive"><span title="See entry on this page at § hard disk drive" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">hard disk drives</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="direct_mapped_cache" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Direct_mapped_cache" class="mw-redirect" title="Direct mapped cache">direct mapped cache</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> where each <a href="#physical_address"><span title="See entry on this page at § physical address" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">physical address</span></a> may only be mapped to one <a href="#cache_line"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache line" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache line</span></a>, indexed using the low bits of the <a href="#address"><span title="See entry on this page at § address" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address</span></a>. Simple but highly prone to allocation conflicts.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="direct_memory_access_(dma)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Direct_memory_access" title="Direct memory access">direct memory access</a> (DMA)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The ability of a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hardware_device&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hardware device (page does not exist)">hardware device</a> such as a <a href="#disk_drive"><span title="See entry on this page at § disk drive" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">disk drive</span></a> or <a href="/wiki/Network_interface" title="Network interface">network interface</a> to access <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a> without intervention from the <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a>,  provided by one or more <a href="/wiki/DMA_channel" class="mw-redirect" title="DMA channel">DMA channels</a> in a system.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="displayport" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/DisplayPort" title="DisplayPort">DisplayPort</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A digital display interface developed by the Video Electronics Standards Association (VESA). The interface is primarily used to connect a video source to a display device such as a computer monitor, though it can also be used to transmit audio, USB, and other forms of data.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="drive_bay" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Drive_bay" title="Drive bay">drive bay</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A standard-sized area within a <a href="#computer_case"><span title="See entry on this page at § computer case" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">computer case</span></a> for adding <a href="#hardware"><span title="See entry on this page at § hardware" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">hardware</span></a> (hard drives, CD drives, etc.) to a computer.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="dual_in-line_memory_module_(dimm)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/DIMM" title="DIMM">dual in-line memory module</a> (DIMM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A series of dynamic random-access memory integrated circuits. These modules are mounted on a printed circuit board and designed for use in personal computers, workstations and servers. Contrast <i><a href="#sIMM"><span title="See entry on this page at § SIMM" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">SIMM</span></a></i>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="dual_issue" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">dual issue</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="/wiki/Superscalar" class="mw-redirect" title="Superscalar">superscalar</a> pipeline capable of executing two instructions simultaneously.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="dynamic_random-access_memory_(dram)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Dynamic_random-access_memory" title="Dynamic random-access memory">dynamic random-access memory</a> (DRAM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of random-access memory that stores each bit of data in a separate capacitor within an integrated circuit and which must be periodically refreshed to retain the stored data.</dd>

</dl>

<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="expansion_bus" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Expansion_bus" class="mw-redirect" title="Expansion bus">expansion bus</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#bus"><span title="See entry on this page at § bus" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">computer bus</span></a> which moves information between the internal hardware of a computer system (including the CPU and RAM) and peripheral devices. It is a collection of wires and <a href="/wiki/Communications_protocol" class="mw-redirect" title="Communications protocol">protocols</a> that allows for the expansion of a computer.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="F">F</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: F">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">

<dt class="glossary" id="floppy_disk_drive" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Floppy_disk_drive" class="mw-redirect" title="Floppy disk drive">floppy disk drive</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A device for reading <a href="#floppy_disk"><span title="See entry on this page at § floppy disk" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">floppy disks</span></a>.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="H">H</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: H">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="hard_disk_drive_(hdd)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Hard_disk_drive" title="Hard disk drive">hard disk drive</a> (HDD)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any non-volatile <a href="#storage_medium"><span title="See entry on this page at § storage medium" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">storage device</span></a> that stores data on rapidly rotating rigid (i.e. hard) platters with magnetic surfaces.</dd>

<dd class="glossary">The physical components of a computer system.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="harvard_architecture" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Harvard_architecture" title="Harvard architecture">Harvard architecture</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#memory_architecture"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory architecture" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory architecture</span></a> where <a href="/wiki/Program_machine_code" class="mw-redirect" title="Program machine code">program machine code</a> and data are held in separate <a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memories</span></a>, more commonly seen in <a href="#microcontroller"><span title="See entry on this page at § microcontroller" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">microcontrollers</span></a> and <a href="/wiki/Digital_signal_processor" title="Digital signal processor">digital signal processors</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="high-definition_multimedia_interface_(hdmi)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/High-Definition_Multimedia_Interface" class="mw-redirect" title="High-Definition Multimedia Interface">High-Definition Multimedia Interface</a> (HDMI)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A compact interface for transferring encrypted uncompressed digital audio and video data to a device such as a computer monitor, video projector or digital television.</dd>

</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="I">I</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: I">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="input_device" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Input_device" title="Input device">input device</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any peripheral equipment used to provide data and control signals to an information processing system.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="input/output_(i/o)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Input/output" title="Input/output">input/output</a> (I/O)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The communication between an information processing system (such as a computer), and the outside world.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="input/output_operations_per_second_(iops)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/IOPS" title="IOPS">Input/Output Operations Per Second</a> (IOPS)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A common performance measurement used to benchmark <a href="/wiki/Computer_storage_device" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer storage device">computer storage devices</a> like <a href="#hard_disk_drive"><span title="See entry on this page at § hard disk drive" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">hard disk drives</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="instruction" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Instruction_(computer_science)" class="mw-redirect" title="Instruction (computer science)">instruction</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A group of several bits in a computer program that contains an <a href="#operation_code"><span title="See entry on this page at § operation code" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">operation code</span></a> and usually one or more <a href="#memory_address"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory address" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory addresses</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="instruction_cache" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Instruction_cache" class="mw-redirect" title="Instruction cache">instruction cache</a></dfn></dt>
<dt class="glossary" id="i-cache" style="margin-top: -0.2em;"><dfn class="glossary">I-cache</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> in a <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPU</span></a> or <a href="#gPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § GPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">GPU</span></a> servicing <a href="#instruction_fetch"><span title="See entry on this page at § instruction fetch" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">instruction fetch</span></a> requests for program code (or <a href="/wiki/Shader" title="Shader">shaders</a> for a <a href="#gPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § GPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">GPU</span></a>), possibly implementing <a href="/wiki/Modified_Harvard_architecture" title="Modified Harvard architecture">modified Harvard architecture</a> if <a href="/wiki/Program_machine_code" class="mw-redirect" title="Program machine code">program machine code</a> is stored in the same <a href="#address_space"><span title="See entry on this page at § address space" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address space</span></a> and <a href="/wiki/Physical_memory" class="mw-redirect" title="Physical memory">physical memory</a> as data.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="instruction_fetch" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">instruction fetch</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A stage in a <a href="#pipeline"><span title="See entry on this page at § pipeline" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">pipeline</span></a> that loads the next <a href="#instruction"><span title="See entry on this page at § instruction" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">instruction</span></a> referred to by the <a href="#program_counter"><span title="See entry on this page at § program counter" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">program counter</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="integrated_circuit" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuit</a></dfn></dt><span id="integrated_circuit"></span>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>chip</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">A miniaturised <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuit" title="Electronic circuit">electronic circuit</a> that has been manufactured in the surface of a thin substrate of <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a> material.</dd>

</dl>

<dt class="glossary" id="magneto-optical_drive" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Magneto-optical_drive" title="Magneto-optical drive">magneto-optical drive</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary"></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="main_memory" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Main_memory" class="mw-redirect" title="Main memory">main memory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The largest <a href="#random-access_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § random-access memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">random-access memory</span></a> in a <a href="/wiki/Memory_hierarchy" title="Memory hierarchy">memory hierarchy</a> (before offline storage) in a computer system; i.e. distinct from <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">caches</span></a> or <a href="#scratchpad_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § scratchpad memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">scratchpads</span></a>; usually consists of <a href="#dRAM"><span title="See entry on this page at § DRAM" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">DRAM</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="mask_rom" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Mask_ROM" title="Mask ROM">mask ROM</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of <a href="#read-only_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § read-only memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">read-only memory</span></a> (ROM) whose contents are programmed by the <a href="#integrated_circuit"><span title="See entry on this page at § integrated circuit" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">integrated circuit</span></a> manufacturer.</dd>

<dd class="glossary">Devices that are used to store data or programs on a temporary or permanent basis for use in an electronic digital computer.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="memory_access_pattern" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Memory_access_pattern" title="Memory access pattern">memory access pattern</a></dfn></dt>

<dt class="glossary" id="memory_address" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Memory_address" title="Memory address">memory address</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The <a href="#address"><span title="See entry on this page at § address" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address</span></a> of a location in a <a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory</span></a> or other <a href="#address_space"><span title="See entry on this page at § address space" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address space</span></a>.</dd>

<dd class="glossary">A memory architecture in a <a href="/wiki/Computer_system" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer system">computer system</a>, e.g. <a href="#nUMA"><span title="See entry on this page at § NUMA" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">NUMA</span></a>, <a href="#uniform_memory_access"><span title="See entry on this page at § uniform memory access" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">uniform memory access</span></a>, <a href="#cOMA"><span title="See entry on this page at § COMA" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">COMA</span></a>, etc.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="mini-vga" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Mini-VGA" title="Mini-VGA">mini-VGA</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Small connectors used on some laptops and other systems in place of the standard <a href="#vGA"><span title="See entry on this page at § VGA" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">VGA</span></a> connector.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="modem" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Modem" title="Modem">modem</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary"></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="modified_harvard_architecture" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Modified_Harvard_architecture" title="Modified Harvard architecture">modified Harvard architecture</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A variation of <a href="#harvard_architecture"><span title="See entry on this page at § Harvard architecture" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">Harvard architecture</span></a> used for most <a href="#cPU"><span title="See entry on this page at § CPU" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">CPUs</span></a> with separate non-coherent <a href="#instruction_cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § instruction cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">instruction</span></a> and <a href="#data_cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § data cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">data caches</span></a> (assuming that code is immutable), but still mirroring the same <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a> <a href="#address_space"><span title="See entry on this page at § address space" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">address space</span></a>, and possibly sharing higher levels of the same <a href="/wiki/Cache_hierarchy" title="Cache hierarchy">cache hierarchy</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="monitor" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_monitor" title="Computer monitor">monitor</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An electronic visual display for computers.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="motherboard" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Motherboard" title="Motherboard">motherboard</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The central printed circuit board (PCB) in many modern computers which holds many of the crucial components of the system, usually while also providing connection space for <a href="#peripheral"><span title="See entry on this page at § peripheral" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">peripherals</span></a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="mouse" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Mouse_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Mouse (computing)">mouse</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#pointing_device"><span title="See entry on this page at § pointing device" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">pointing device</span></a> that functions by detecting two-dimensional motion relative to its supporting surface; motion is usually mapped to a cursor in <a href="/wiki/Screen_space" class="mw-redirect" title="Screen space">screen space</a>; typically used to control a <a href="/wiki/Graphical_user_interface" title="Graphical user interface">graphical user interface</a> on a <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktop computer</a> or for <a href="/wiki/Computer-aided_design" title="Computer-aided design">CAD</a>, etc.</dd>

</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="N">N</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: N">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="network" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">network</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A collection of computers and other devices connected by communications channels, e.g. by <a href="/wiki/Ethernet" title="Ethernet">Ethernet</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wireless_network" title="Wireless network">wireless networking</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="network_interface_controller" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Network_interface_controller" title="Network interface controller">network interface controller</a></dfn></dt>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>LAN card</b> or <b>network card</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary"><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="network_on_a_chip_(noc)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Network_on_a_chip" title="Network on a chip">network on a chip</a> (NOC)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#network"><span title="See entry on this page at § network" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">computer network</span></a> on a single <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_chip" class="mw-redirect" title="Semiconductor chip">semiconductor chip</a>, connecting <a href="#processing_element"><span title="See entry on this page at § processing element" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">processing elements</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Fixed_function_unit" class="mw-redirect" title="Fixed function unit">fixed function units</a>, or even <a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memories</span></a> and <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">caches</span></a>. Increasingly common in <a href="/wiki/System_on_a_chip" title="System on a chip">system on a chip</a> designs.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="non-uniform_memory_access_(numa)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Non-uniform_memory_access" title="Non-uniform memory access">non-uniform memory access</a> (NUMA)</dfn></dt><span id="NUMA"></span>
<dd class="glossary"></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="non-volatile_memory" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Non-volatile_memory" title="Non-volatile memory">non-volatile memory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary"><a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § Memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory</span></a> that can retain the stored data even when not powered, as opposed to <a href="#volatile_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § volatile memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">volatile memory</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="non-volatile_random-access_memory" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Non-volatile_random-access_memory" title="Non-volatile random-access memory">non-volatile random-access memory</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary"><a href="#random-access_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § random-access memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">Random-access memory</span></a> (RAM) that retains its data when power is turned off.</dd>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="O">O</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: O">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="operating_system" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Operating_system" title="Operating system">operating system</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The set of software that manages computer <a href="#hardware_resource"><span title="See entry on this page at § hardware resource" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">hardware resources</span></a> and provides common services for <a href="/wiki/Computer_program" title="Computer program">computer programs</a>, typically loaded by the <a href="/wiki/BIOS" title="BIOS">BIOS</a> on <a href="/wiki/Booting" title="Booting">booting</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="operation_code" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Opcode" title="Opcode">operation code</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Several bits in a <a href="/wiki/Computer_program" title="Computer program">computer program</a> <a href="#instruction"><span title="See entry on this page at § instruction" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">instruction</span></a> that specify which operation to perform.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="optical_disc_drive" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Optical_disc_drive" title="Optical disc drive">optical disc drive</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of <a href="#disk_drive"><span title="See entry on this page at § disk drive" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">disk drive</span></a> that uses <a href="/wiki/Laser_light" class="mw-redirect" title="Laser light">laser light</a> or <a href="/wiki/Electromagnetic_wave" class="mw-redirect" title="Electromagnetic wave">electromagnetic waves</a> near the <a href="/wiki/Light_spectrum" class="mw-redirect" title="Light spectrum">light spectrum</a> as part of the process of reading or writing data to or from <a href="/wiki/Optical_disc" title="Optical disc">optical discs</a>.</dd>

</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="P">P</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: P">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="pen_drive" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Pen_drive" class="mw-redirect" title="Pen drive">pen drive</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Another name for a <a href="#uSB_flash_drive"><span title="See entry on this page at § USB flash drive" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">USB flash drive</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="peripheral" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Peripheral" title="Peripheral">peripheral</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any device attached to a computer but not part of it.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="personal_computer_(pc)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computer</a> (PC)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any <a href="/wiki/General-purpose_computer" class="mw-redirect" title="General-purpose computer">general-purpose computer</a> whose size, capabilities, and original sales price make it useful for individuals, and which is intended to be operated directly by an end user, with no intervening computer operator.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="power_supply" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Power_supply" title="Power supply">power supply</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A unit of the computer that converts mains AC to low-voltage regulated DC for the power of all the computer components.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="power_supply_unit_(psu)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Power_supply_unit_(computer)" title="Power supply unit (computer)">power supply unit</a> (PSU)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Converts <a href="/wiki/Mains_electricity" title="Mains electricity">mains AC</a> to low-voltage regulated <a href="/wiki/DC_power" class="mw-redirect" title="DC power">DC power</a> for the internal components of a computer. Modern personal computers universally use <a href="/wiki/Switched-mode_power_supply" title="Switched-mode power supply">switched-mode power supplies</a>. Some <a href="#power_supply"><span title="See entry on this page at § power supply" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">power supplies</span></a> have a manual switch for selecting input voltage, while others automatically adapt to the mains voltage.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="prefetch" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">prefetch</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The process of pre-loading <a href="/wiki/Instruction_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Instruction (computing)">instructions</a> or data into a <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> ahead of time, either under manual control via <a href="/wiki/Prefetch_instruction" class="mw-redirect" title="Prefetch instruction">prefetch instructions</a> or automatically by a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Prefetch_unit&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Prefetch unit (page does not exist)">prefetch unit</a> which may use runtime heuristics to predict the future <a href="/wiki/Memory_access_pattern" title="Memory access pattern">memory access pattern</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="prefetching" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">prefetching</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The pre-loading of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Instructions_(computing)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Instructions (computing) (page does not exist)">instructions</a> or <a href="/wiki/Data_(computing)" title="Data (computing)">data</a> before either is needed by dedicated <a href="/wiki/Cache_control_instruction" title="Cache control instruction">cache control instructions</a> or predictive hardware, to mitigate <a href="/wiki/Latency_(engineering)" title="Latency (engineering)">latency</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="printer" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Printer_(computing)" title="Printer (computing)">printer</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#peripheral"><span title="See entry on this page at § peripheral" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">peripheral</span></a> which produces a text or graphics of documents stored in electronic form, usually on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Physical_print_media&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Physical print media (page does not exist)">physical print media</a> such as paper or transparencies.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="process_node" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">process node</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Refers to a level of <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a> <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor_device_fabrication" title="Semiconductor device fabrication">manufacturing technology</a>, one of several successive <a href="/wiki/Transistor" title="Transistor">transistor</a> shrinks.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="processing_element" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">processing element</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="/wiki/Electronic_circuit" title="Electronic circuit">electronic circuit</a> (either a <a href="/wiki/Microprocessor" title="Microprocessor">microprocessor</a> or an internal component of one) that may function autonomously or under external control, performing <a href="/wiki/Arithmetic_and_logic_operation" class="mw-redirect" title="Arithmetic and logic operation">arithmetic and logic operations</a> on <a href="/wiki/Data_(computing)" title="Data (computing)">data</a>, possibly containing <a href="#local_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § local memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">local memory</span></a>, and possibly connected to other <a href="#processing_element">processing elements</a> via a <a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">network</a>, <a href="/wiki/Network_on_a_chip" title="Network on a chip">network on a chip</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Cache_hierarchy" title="Cache hierarchy">cache hierarchy</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="processor_node" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary">processor node</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="/wiki/Processor_(computing)" title="Processor (computing)">processor</a> in a <a href="/w/index.php?title=Multiprocessor_system&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Multiprocessor system (page does not exist)">multiprocessor system</a> or <a href="/wiki/Cluster_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Cluster (computing)">cluster</a>, connected by dedicated communication channels or a <a href="/wiki/Network_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Network (computing)">network</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="programmable_read-only_memory_(prom)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Programmable_read-only_memory" title="Programmable read-only memory">programmable read-only memory</a> (PROM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of <a href="/wiki/Non-volatile_memory" title="Non-volatile memory">non-volatile memory</a> chip that may be programmed after the device is constructed.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="programmer" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Programmer_(hardware)" title="Programmer (hardware)">programmer</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any electronic equipment that arranges written software to configure programmable <a href="/wiki/Non-volatile_memory" title="Non-volatile memory">non-volatile</a> <a href="/wiki/Integrated_circuit" title="Integrated circuit">integrated circuits</a> (called programmable devices) such as  <a href="/wiki/EPROM" title="EPROM">EPROMs</a>, <a href="/wiki/EEPROM" title="EEPROM">EEPROMs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Flash_Memory" class="mw-redirect" title="Flash Memory">Flashes</a>, <a href="/wiki/MultiMediaCard" title="MultiMediaCard">eMMC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Magnetoresistive_random-access_memory" title="Magnetoresistive random-access memory">MRAM</a>, FRAM, <a href="/wiki/Non-volatile_random-access_memory" title="Non-volatile random-access memory">NV RAM</a>, <a href="/wiki/Programmable_Array_Logic" title="Programmable Array Logic">PALs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Field_Programmable_Gate_Array" class="mw-redirect" title="Field Programmable Gate Array">FPGAs</a> or <a href="/wiki/Programmable_logic" class="mw-redirect" title="Programmable logic">programmable logic</a> circuits.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="pci_express_(pcie)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/PCI_Express" title="PCI Express">PCI Express</a> (PCIe)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="#expansion_bus"><span title="See entry on this page at § expansion bus" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">expansion bus</span></a> standard designed to replace the older <a href="#pCI"><span title="See entry on this page at § PCI" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">PCI</span></a>, <a href="#pCI-X"><span title="See entry on this page at § PCI-X" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">PCI-X</span></a>, and <a href="#aGP"><span title="See entry on this page at § AGP" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">AGP</span></a> bus standards.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="pci-extended_(pci-x)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/PCI-eXtended" class="mw-redirect" title="PCI-eXtended">PCI-eXtended</a> (PCI-X)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">An <a href="#expansion_bus"><span title="See entry on this page at § expansion bus" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">expansion bus</span></a> and <a href="#expansion_card"><span title="See entry on this page at § expansion card" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">expansion card</span></a> standard that enhances the 32-bit PCI Local Bus for higher bandwidth demanded by servers.</dd>

</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="R">R</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: R">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="redundant_array_of_independent_disks_(raid)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Redundant_Array_of_Independent_Disks" class="mw-redirect" title="Redundant Array of Independent Disks">Redundant Array of Independent Disks</a> (RAID)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any of various data storage schemes that can divide and replicate data across multiple hard disk drives in order to increase reliability, allow faster access, or both.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="random-access_memory_(ram)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Random-access_memory" title="Random-access memory">random-access memory</a> (RAM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of computer data storage that allows data items to be accessed (<a href="/wiki/Read_(computer)" class="mw-redirect" title="Read (computer)">read</a> or written) in almost the same amount of time irrespective of the physical location of data inside the memory. RAM contains <a href="/wiki/Multiplexer" title="Multiplexer">multiplexing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demultiplexing" class="mw-redirect" title="Demultiplexing">demultiplexing</a> circuitry to connect the data lines to the addressed storage for reading or writing the entry. Usually more than one bit of storage is accessed by the same address, and RAM devices often have multiple data lines and are said to be '8-bit' or '16-bit' etc. devices. In today's technology, random-access memory takes the form of <a href="#integrated_circuit"><span title="See entry on this page at § integrated circuit" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">integrated circuits</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="read-only_memory_(rom)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Read-only_memory" title="Read-only memory">read-only memory</a> (ROM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of memory chip that retains its data when its power supply is switched off.</dd>

</dl>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="S">S</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: S">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<dl class="glossary">
<dt class="glossary" id="server" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">server</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A computer which may be used to provide services to clients.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="software" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Software" title="Software">software</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">Any <a href="/wiki/Computer_program" title="Computer program">computer program</a> or other kind of information that can be read and/or written by a computer.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="single_in-line_memory_module_(simm)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Single_in-line_memory_module" class="mw-redirect" title="Single in-line memory module">single in-line memory module</a> (SIMM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of <a href="/wiki/Memory_module" title="Memory module">memory module</a> containing <a href="#random-access_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § random-access memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">random-access memory</span></a> used in computers from the early 1980s to the late 1990s. Contrast <i><a href="#dIMM"><span title="See entry on this page at § DIMM" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">DIMM</span></a></i>.</dd>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg/220px-Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg/330px-Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5e/Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg/440px-Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3531" data-file-height="2317" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vertex_2_Solid_State_Drive_by_OCZ-top_oblique_PNr%C2%B00307.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A 2.5-inch <a href="#solid-state_drive"><span title="See entry on this page at § solid-state drive" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">solid-state drive</span></a> that can be used in <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">laptops</a> and <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktop computers</a></div></div></div>
<dt class="glossary" id="solid-state_drive" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Solid-state_drive" title="Solid-state drive">solid-state drive</a></dfn></dt>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>solid-state disk</b> or <b>electronic disk</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">Any data storage device that uses integrated circuit assemblies as memory to store data persistently. Though they are sometimes referred to as solid-state disks, these devices contain neither an actual disk nor a drive motor to spin a disk.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="static_random-access_memory_(sram)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Static_random-access_memory" title="Static random-access memory">static random-access memory</a> (SRAM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductor</a> <a href="#memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">memory</span></a> that uses bistable latching circuitry to store each bit. The term static differentiates it from <a href="#dRAM"><span title="See entry on this page at § DRAM" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">DRAM</span></a>, which must be periodically refreshed.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="sound_card" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Sound_card" title="Sound card">sound card</a></dfn></dt>
<p class="glossary-hatnote"><span role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable selfref">Also <b>audio card</b>.</span></p>
<dd class="glossary">An internal <a href="#expansion_card"><span title="See entry on this page at § expansion card" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">expansion card</span></a> that facilitates economical input and output of <a href="/wiki/Sound" title="Sound">audio signals</a> to and from a computer under control of computer programs.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="storage_device" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Storage_device_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Storage device (computing)">storage device</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary"></dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="synchronous_dynamic_random-access_memory_(sdram)" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Synchronous_dynamic_random-access_memory" title="Synchronous dynamic random-access memory">synchronous dynamic random-access memory</a> (SDRAM)</dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A type of <a href="#dynamic_random_access_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § dynamic random access memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">dynamic random access memory</span></a> that is synchronized with the <a href="/wiki/System_bus" title="System bus">system bus</a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="superdisk" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/SuperDisk" title="SuperDisk">SuperDisk</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A high-speed, high-capacity alternative to the 90 mm (3.5 in), 1.44 <a href="/wiki/Megabyte" title="Megabyte">MB</a> floppy disk. The SuperDisk hardware was created by 3M's storage products group <a href="/wiki/Imation" class="mw-redirect" title="Imation">Imation</a> in 1997.</dd>

</dl>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="U">U</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Glossary_of_computer_hardware_terms&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: U">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</dd>

<dd class="glossary">A <a href="/wiki/Video_camera" title="Video camera">video camera</a> that feeds its images in <a href="/w/index.php?title=Real_time_(computing)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Real time (computing) (page does not exist)">real time</a> to a computer or <a href="/wiki/Computer_network" title="Computer network">computer network</a>, often via <a href="#uSB"><span title="See entry on this page at § USB" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">USB</span></a>, <a href="/wiki/Ethernet" title="Ethernet">Ethernet</a>, or Wi-Fi.<sup id="cite_ref-Shelly_Vermaat_2008_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Shelly_Vermaat_2008-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></dd>

<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> where <a href="/w/index.php?title=Store_operations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Store operations (page does not exist)">store operations</a> are buffered in <a href="#cache_line"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache line" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache lines</span></a>, only reaching <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a> when the entire cache line is <a href="#cache_eviction"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache eviction" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">evicted</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="write_through_cache" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Write_through_cache" class="mw-redirect" title="Write through cache">write through cache</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">A <a href="#cache"><span title="See entry on this page at § cache" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">cache</span></a> where <a href="/w/index.php?title=Store_operations&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Store operations (page does not exist)">store operations</a> are immediately written to the underlying <a href="#main_memory"><span title="See entry on this page at § main memory" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">main memory</span></a>.</dd>

<dt class="glossary" id="working_set" style="margin-top: 0.4em;"><dfn class="glossary"><a href="/wiki/Working_set" title="Working set">working set</a></dfn></dt>
<dd class="glossary">The set of data used by a <a href="#processor"><span title="See entry on this page at § processor" class="glossary-link-internal" style="border-bottom: 1px dashed #86a1ff; color:initial;" id="">processor</span></a> during a certain time interval, which should ideally fit into a <a href="/wiki/CPU_cache" title="CPU cache">CPU cache</a> for optimum performance.</dd>
